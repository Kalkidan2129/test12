## Project Overview

This project involved creating a detailed sales dashboard using Power BI to analyze Walmart store sales data from February 2010 to October 2012. It focused on understanding sales performance and trends across multiple stores during the economic recovery following the 2008 recession. The dashboard incorporated data modeling, DAX measures, and conditional columns to provide actionable retail insights.

---

## Business Problem

Walmart stores, like many discount retailers, faced fluctuating sales during the recovery period after the 2008 recession. Understanding year-over-year sales trends was critical to identifying factors affecting store performance and making data-driven decisions to improve sales. The challenge was to analyze and visualize sales data effectively to uncover these insights.

---

## Objective

- Analyze year-over-year sales trends across multiple Walmart stores.
- Develop a dynamic Power BI dashboard for visualizing sales performance.
- Incorporate economic indicators like unemployment rates and holiday periods to contextualize sales data.

---

## Tools & Technologies

- Power BI Desktop
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
- Excel
- Power Query Editor
- CSV Data Files

---

## Project Workflow

- Import Walmart sales CSV data into Power BI Desktop.
- Create calendar and refresh date tables for time intelligence.
- Establish relationships between sales and calendar tables.
- Develop conditional columns for holiday flags and unemployment rates.
- Build DAX measures to calculate total sales, average sales, and year-over-year growth.

---

## Key Insights

- Sales showed variability across stores during the post-recession recovery period.
- Holiday weeks and unemployment rates had measurable impacts on weekly sales.
- Year-over-year sales growth highlighted stores recovering at different rates.
- Data modeling and relationships enabled accurate time-based sales comparisons.
